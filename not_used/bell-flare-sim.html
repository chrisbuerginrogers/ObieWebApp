The physics

The bell transforms the radiation impedance at its mouth back through the flare. At bell-mouth radius a₂, the resistive radiation term is:

Z_rad = ρc · R₁(2ka₂)

where R₁ involves Bessel functions. The area ratio (a₂/a₁)² scales this back to the tube throat, giving effective impedance ratio:

r = R₁(2ka₂) · (a₂/a₁)²

Perfect matching (r = 1) gives T_energy = 100%.

Energy coefficients

r_p = (Z₂ − Z₁) / (Z₁ + Z₂) R_energy = r_p² T_energy = 4r / (1 + r)²

At low frequency, ka₂ ≪ 1 and R₁ → 0, so r → 0 and almost all energy reflects. At high frequency, R₁ → 1 and the bell area ratio alone determines matching. This is why larger bells help most at low frequencies — the regime where brass instruments live.
